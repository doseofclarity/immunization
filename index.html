Fundamentals of Immunization

Active immunity: Body produces antibodies after exposure to antigen (vaccine or natural infection). Long‑lasting.

Passive immunity: Received antibodies (maternal, immunoglobulin). Temporary.

Herd immunity: High vaccination rate protects unvaccinated individuals.

Vaccine types: Live attenuated (MMR, Varicella, LAIV), inactivated (IPV, HepA), subunit/recombinant (HPV, HepB), toxoid (DTaP), conjugate (Hib, PCV13).

Herd immunity threshold depends on disease infectivity (R₀)

Contraindication to live vaccines
Pregnancy, severe immunosuppression, anaphylaxis to previous dose or component.

Adult & Special Populations

  • Td/Tdap: Tdap every 10 years; one dose Tdap for adults who never received (then Td every 10y).
  • Influenza: Annual for all adults, especially high‑risk.
  • Pneumococcal: PCV15/20 or PPSV23 for adults ≥65 or high‑risk conditions.
  • Zoster (shingles): RZV (Shingrix) at age ≥50 years (two doses).
  • COVID‑19: Updated boosters as recommended.
  • Pregnancy: Tdap (27–36 weeks each pregnancy), influenza (any trimester).

Nursing Role & Adverse Event Management

Pre‑vaccination:

  • Screen contraindications/precautions
  • Obtain informed consent
  • Educate about expected side effects

Administration:

  • Use correct needle length, site (vastus lateralis for infants; deltoid for older children/adults)
  • Separate injection sites by ≥1 inch

Adverse events:

  • Common: Pain, redness, low‑grade fever, fussiness
  • Serious (rare): Anaphylaxis (epinephrine ready), febrile seizures (MMR, DTaP), Guillain‑Barré (rare)

Vaccine storage:

Refrigerated 2–8°C, frozen -15°C or colder; monitor temperature daily.

Mnemonics & Quick Review

Infant vaccines at 2 months: "DIP R Him" – DTaP, IPV, PCV13, RV, Hib (HepB at birth)
Contraindications to live vaccines: "PREGS" – Pregnancy, severe immunosuppression, anaphylaxis, recent immunoglobulin.
Adolescent vaccines (11–12y): "THM" – Tdap, HPV, MenACWY.
Pregnancy vaccines: "T.I.P." – Tdap (27‑36w), Influenza, (Pneumococcal if indicated).
